Peace by Piece

A simulation game exploring war, peace, and everything in between—grounded in academic research across security studies, political science, anthropology, economics, and sociology.

The game simulates conflicts in Mali, a country often seen as especially hard to stabilize because relationships between factions are so layered and complex. It combines how groups in northern Mali actually differ—shaped by religion, identities, history, and traditions—with their motivations, resources, power dynamics, and political orientations.

Six party interests are represented on the table. Players compete, forge alliances, cooperate, and betray one another, much as in real life. International actors receive tools to intervene, seek resolutions, and support peacebuilding and state formation.

Each round introduces new, potentially high-reward challenges. Scenarios let you test combinations of solutions that might apply in the real world. Player feedback has been strongly positive, and sessions also yield rich material for academic research.

Players reflect on questions such as

  • Is peace in Mali possible?
  • How can opposing factions be reconciled?
  • Are general elections feasible and desirable?
  • Will a constitution help stabilize the country?

Nothing is predetermined: the sandbox design stays open-ended, with no fixed outcomes—valuable for university teaching, researchers testing hypotheses, and practitioners evaluating strategies before implementation.

Developed in cooperation with the University of Warsaw. · Grand Prix, 2019 Serious Game Design Competition, International Simulation & Gaming Association (ISAGA).

  • Tabletop simulation
  • 6–30 players
  • 3–6 hours
  • Sandbox / open outcomes

Forest Planting

A simulation experience that helps organizations, leaders, and teams start, grow, and multiply—using the forest as a living metaphor for sustainable expansion.

“The exchange between the two tree species was dynamic…and so, they forged their duality into oneness, making a forest.”

— Dr. Suzanne Simard, The Hidden Life of Trees

Why Forest Planting?

A forest is an ecosystem that moves together—designed to grow, multiply, and carry what it needs for sustainability. What if you stopped focusing only on “your tree” and began dreaming of growing a forest across your organization and community?

We need to play with ideas

Conferences and books flood us with concepts; leaders still need a safe place to experiment. Forest Planting lets teams play with multiplication principles and turn insight into workable plans.

We need to play with others

Serious play is real: our brains open up when we’re having fun together. As George Bernard Shaw put it, “You don’t stop playing because you grow old, you grow old because you stop playing.” Play and relationships build capacity for change—and reveal how your team really collaborates under pressure.

The game — what you will experience

  1. Simulation. A beautifully produced board game created by experienced leaders. In a forest setting, your team finds strategies to plant and expand: four tree types, each with strengths and weaknesses, compete for limited space and resources to multiply into unreached areas of the board. Nature and team dynamics test vision, strategy, execution, and emotion. Years of research anchor the design in real forest principles and in how teams and organizations plant and multiply.
  2. Debriefing. The leverage is in the debrief—structured to surface learnings and next steps. Using facilitation approaches such as Results-based Conversations, we connect the table experience to your organization and help the team produce concrete actions for implementation.

How we can support you

We don’t aim to lecture—we put you through an experience that draws out knowledge you already have and makes it usable. Forest Planting can stand alone or deepen training you already run. We personalize the flow to your priorities.

Entrepreneurship

Starting a venture is energizing and demanding. The simulation helps clarify vision and strategy and sparks practical discussion about overcoming growth challenges.

Leadership and team dynamics

Mechanics and social play surface personalities, emotions, stress responses, and communication patterns. Many teams learn more in a few hours of play than in a year of routine meetings.

Debriefing can explore, for example

  • Are we maximizing strengths and minimizing weaknesses as a team?
  • What are the keys to effective communication?
  • How do we break silos and build a culture of collaboration?

Tailoring to your needs

Forest Planting is versatile: we’ve used it with businesses and non-profits—for growth, new markets, and imagination-stretching strategy. Share your goals and we can shape a Forest Planting experience around them.

  • Tabletop simulation
  • 4–40 players
  • 3–5 hours
  • Facilitated debrief
